Georgia General Assembly

About the Assembly

Georgia's annual legislative session is 40 days at the beginning of the year. During that time, our state Senators and Representatives gather inside the Capitol in Atlanta to discuss bills and vote to create new laws. Together they make up the Georgia General Assembly, which is the legislative branch of our state government.

The General Assembly is made up of two chambers: the Senate and the House of Representatives. There are 56 state Senators and 180 state Representatives, each representing a district of voters across Georgia. Georgia House and Senate members serve two-year terms, with all seats up for election every two years.

Many counties are served by multiple districts. If you are unsure which district you're in, use the GA Secretary of State My Voter Page and scroll to the bottom to see your districts. Select a member to view details about their time in office.
