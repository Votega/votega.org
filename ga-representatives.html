Georgia General Assembly

Georgia's annual legislative session is 40 days at the beginning of the year. During that time, our state Senators and Representatives gather inside the Capitol in Atlanta to discuss bills and vote to create new laws. Together they make up the Georgia General Assembly, which is the legislative branch of our state government. The General Assembly is made up of two chambers: the Senate and the House of Representatives. There are 56 state Senators and 180 state Representatives, each representing a district of voters across Georgia.

Find Your GA State Legislator

Select your County and Chamber (Senate or House). The districts serving your county will appear in the list. Many counties are served by multiple districts, and some districts serve multiple counties.

If you are unsure which district you're in, you can use the GA Secretary of State My Voter Page tool. Enter your information and scroll to the bottom of the page to see your districts (both at the federal and state level). Then, you can identify which member and/or candidate is in your district.
