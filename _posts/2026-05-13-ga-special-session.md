title: Georgia Special Legislative Session
subtitle: Governor Kemp calls Special Session of General Assembly
---

A special session of the Georgia General Assembly will begin on Wednesday, June 17, 2026 for the purposes of:
1. Consider enacting, revising, repealing, or amending the law for the division of the State into appropriate districts from which members of the Georgia State Senate, the Georgia State House of Representatives, the U.S. Congress House of Representatives or any other state office elected by district. 
2. To address issues created by the July 1, 2026 effective date for changes to GA Code Section 21-2-379.23 which was enacted by <a href= "https://www.legis.ga.gov/api/legislation/document/20232024/229578" target="_blank">Georgia Senate Bill 189</a>.

Plain language: The State legislature will review, likely revise, and redraw district maps which may disenfranchise voters (primarily minority voters). This is a direct result of the Supreme Court decision in <a href= "https://www.oyez.org/cases/2025/24-109/" target="_blank">Louisiana v. Callais</a> (Apr 29, 2026). Any changes made will take effect for the 2028 election cycle.

Georgia is now following the lead of States such as: Alabama, Tennessee, Louisiana, and others. However, for the time being it does not appear that any changes in Georgia will be effective for the upcoming Primary Election on May 19, 2026 or the General Election on November 3, 2026.
