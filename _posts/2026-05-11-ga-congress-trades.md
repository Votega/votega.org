Georgia's six members of Congress have filed **1,081 stock trades** since taking office — and federal law requires every one of them to be disclosed publicly.

Under the STOCK Act (Stop Trading on Congressional Knowledge Act), members of Congress must report stock transactions within 45 days of the trade. The law was passed in 2012 after investigations found lawmakers trading in sectors directly affected by legislation they were writing. The idea is simple: if you're voting on policy that moves markets, the public deserves to know what you're buying and selling.

Here's a snapshot of what Georgia's delegation has been trading:

**Marjorie Taylor Greene (GA-14)** leads the delegation by a wide margin with **545 disclosed trades**, including repeated transactions in Amazon, Tesla, AMD, Alphabet, and UPS.

**Richard W. Allen (GA-12)** comes in second with **279 trades**, with SouthState Bank Corporation appearing as his most traded holding — a regional Georgia bank.

**Austin Scott (GA-08)** logged **99 trades**, with a notable concentration in clean energy stocks: FuelCell Energy, Plug Power, Bloom Energy, and GE Aerospace — companies whose valuations are closely tied to federal energy subsidies and regulation.

**Richard McCormick (GA-07)** filed **81 trades**, including L3Harris Technologies, a major U.S. defense contractor.

**Michael A. Collins (GA-10)** and **Earl Carter (GA-01)** round out the delegation with 42 and 35 trades respectively.
